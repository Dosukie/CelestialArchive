Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

The Celestial Archive is a curated collection of space exploration imagery, astronomical discoveries, and scientific data from various space agencies and observatories around the world. We organize and present this information to make space exploration accessible to everyone.

We add new content weekly, featuring the latest discoveries from space missions, telescope observations, and scientific research. Subscribe to our newsletter to get the latest discoveries delivered to your inbox, or follow us on social media for daily updates.

We are an independent project and are not officially affiliated with any space agency. However, we rely on and deeply appreciate the publicly available data and imagery from NASA, ESA, JAXA, and other space organizations worldwide.

Usage & Licensing

Absolutely! All media is sourced from public domain or freely licensed archives. Please credit the original source, which we provide on all content pages. For educational use, this is typically all that's required.

Most content is available for commercial use since it comes from public domain sources. However, please check the specific licensing information provided with each image or dataset, as some may have restrictions.

Contributing & Community

We welcome contributions! You can suggest new content, report errors, or help with translations. Contact us through the form above or reach out via social media. We especially appreciate tips about new discoveries or interesting space-related content.

We're currently developing an API for developers who want to integrate our content into their applications. Join our developer newsletter or Discord community for updates on the API release and documentation.
